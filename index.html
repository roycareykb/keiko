About

A name is a spell

My mother named me after Japanese pianist and composer Keiko Matsui. Depending on how you write it in kanji, Keiko can mean happy child, respectful child, scenic child, sunlight child, or open child. In a different context entirely, Keiko means practice, to contemplate the past and to reflect on old ways.

I now see that I carry all of those meanings, as my mother imprinted her deepest hope for me, and I intuit even herself, inside my name. A name is powerful because it is also a spell. She must have known that by naming me Keiko, I would manifest in every moment my name is spoken out loud.

This might be my first experience of what Thich Nhat Hanh calls interbeing: the understanding that we are all deeply connected. So when you say my name, you too are participating in realizing my mom’s dream for me. And because we inter-are, it is a spell for you too: may you be happy, joyful, open, contemplative and reflective.

I’m someone who deeply cares about people, about our peace, our sense of freedom, and our capacity to live in a way that actually feels authentic. I’ve spent over a decade creating space for people, 1:1 and in community, to come back to freedom in their work, their relationships, their creative life, and their sense of who they are.

I’ve formally guided 40+ people through some of life’s biggest crossroads: career changes, the end of relationships, grief, creative blocks, spiritual questioning, and the quiet unraveling that happens when you know where you are isn’t it anymore, but you just don’t yet know what’s next. I never tell people what to do. I hold the space. I ask the questions that help you hear yourself more clearly.

I experience deep grief, deep love, and the particular kind of disorientation that comes from starting over again and again. All of it is woven into how I show up.

Community will always be central to my offerings. I’m inviting people in and holding space where transformation can emerge, bridging inner wisdom and knowing to usher in the world we’re reimagining: a better world for all of us, especially those who come after us.

integrating through practice

Regeneration over Extraction
Building with people, not off of them. Honoring the wholeness of what each person brings to every collaboration.
Integration over Information
Embodying knowledge, not just collecting it. Letting what's learned move through the body and actually change something.
Invitation over Centering
Creating space, and not dominating it. Inviting people into their own wisdom instead.
Interbeing over Dogma
Everything is interconnected. Refusing to collapse complex tensions into easy answers.
Embodiment over Theory
Ground ideas in lived experience, sensation, and story. I am a dancer. Knowledge that doesn't move through the body doesn't move me.
Flow over Force
Trusting what wants to emerge. Honoring the capacity of everyone involved rather than pushing past what's ready.
Accessibility over Elitism
Making complexity accessible without watering it down. Deep healing work shouldn't require specific vocabulary or credentials to enter.
Open Source over Proprietary
Sharing what's learned freely. Hoarding wisdom doesn't serve the collective, passing it forward does.
Have you ever felt like something inside you knows this isn’t quite right? That’s usually where we begin.

Inyeon

Co-Founder · Since 2022

Much of my work is about creating the conditions for people to see themselves clearly, without shame, without performance, without being rushed toward an answer. Inyeon is a journaling platform inspired by inyeon (인연), the Korean concept of invisible threads between people whose paths were always meant to cross. Voice-first. Compassionately rooted.

Inyeon reflects your awareness back without adding meaning, surfaces compassionate patterns in your reflections, and gets quieter as you develop more compassion.

We’re three years in, co-creating intentionally with a team of five. Sitting with hard questions about AI, self-reflection, post-capitalist economies, and what a tool can and cannot honestly hold. We don’t have clean answers yet. We’re open-sourcing what we learn.

Co-founded with Tobi Olofintuyi.
